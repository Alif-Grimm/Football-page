League Rules

  • Matches are 6/7 a side. Roll-on, roll-off subs are permitted.
  • Matches kick off at different times depending on the venue. For every 5 minutes a team is late, they lose game time and the opposing team gains a goal advantage.
  • Each season, teams play each other twice. The bottom two teams in the Premiership get relegated, and the top two in the Championship get promoted. Champions win medals or trophies.
  • Fees are charged for the season but can be paid weekly at £45 per match. No deposit is required but is subject to the league's discretion.
  • The referee’s decision is final, and overhead height is allowed. Free kicks, throw-ins, and corners must adhere to specific rules, including opposition players standing four yards away.
  • Deliberate fouls near the area result in a sin-bin penalty and specific free-kick setups.
  • Shin pads are mandatory, and players without them may be barred from playing. Metal blades or studs are not allowed.
  • Players cannot enter the goal areas to gain an advantage, and specific rules apply to goalkeeper distribution and ball play.
  • Substitutions must be made without gaining a tactical advantage and are allowed only at set plays.
  • Slide tackles are strictly prohibited for safety reasons.
  • The site prohibits drugs, alcohol, or smoking. Violations may result in bans or penalties.
  • Teams must complete the season or provide 4 weeks' notice to withdraw. Fees for forfeited matches must be covered.
  • Ringers (substitute players from other teams) can only be used under specific conditions. Abuse of this rule may result in penalties or forfeits.
  • Officials must be treated with respect. Any abuse will be reported to the FA and may result in bans.
  • If a match is abandoned due to lack of players, the opposing team will be awarded a victory by one goal or greater goal difference, depending on the match score.

Important Notes:

Please ensure your team adheres to these rules to maintain the integrity and enjoyment of the league. Violations may lead to penalties, forfeits, or bans.
